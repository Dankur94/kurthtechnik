Our Approach

Your child builds a real indoor drone with a mechanical gripper arm. There's no traditional programming — kids describe what they want and AI writes the code (vibe coding). They never hear the word "math" — they calculate because they must, not because they should. Every wrong calculation has a visible consequence: the drone tips, the gripper misses.

Phase 1 — Assembly: "I'm building a drone"
Mission 01–03
Step 01
Assemble the Frame
Identify parts from the BOM. Mount the F330 frame arms to the center plate. Attach the landing gear. Count screws, check symmetry.
AI: Identifies parts from photos, explains assembly order
Mathematics
Counting & sorting Symmetry (P4 Geo) Length: mm/cm (P4)
Systems Thinking
Part–whole relationships Sequential assembly logic
Problem-Solving
Matching parts to diagram
Step 02
Mount Motors & ESCs
Install 4 brushless motors (CW+CCW), connect ESCs. Identify motor rotation direction. Solder XT60 connectors.
AI: Generates wiring diagrams, checks motor direction logic
Mathematics
Multiplication: 4 × $8.50 (P4) Patterns: CW/CCW alternating Weight: g/kg (P4)
Systems Thinking
Input → output (power → spin) Cause & effect (wrong direction = flip)
Creativity
Cable management decisions
Step 03
Connect Flight Controller & Receiver
Wire the F411 flight controller. Bind the FlySky transmitter to receiver. Configure basic settings in Betaflight.
AI: Explains protocol settings, helps debug connection issues
Mathematics
Reading tables (P4 Data) Channel numbers 1–6
Systems Thinking
Signal flow: Tx → Rx → FC → ESC → Motor System dependencies
Problem-Solving
Debugging: "Motor 3 doesn't spin — why?" Systematic elimination
Phase 2 — First Flights: "I'm flying!"
Mission 04–06
Step 04
Pre-Flight Check & Weight Budget
Weigh the drone on a kitchen scale. Calculate total weight vs. max thrust. Check: can it fly? Thrust-to-weight ratio must be > 1.5:1.
AI: Calculates thrust margins, visualises weight distribution
Mathematics
Addition: 380g + 120g + 130g (P4) Ratios: 2.4kg ÷ 1.13kg (P5) Weight units g/kg (P4) Inequalities: > 1.5 (P5)
Systems Thinking
Constraint analysis (weight budget) Trade-offs: heavier = less agile
Step 05
Hover & Land on Target
First takeoff. Hold hover at 1m. Land on a 50cm target zone. Use a floor grid with coordinates. Track flight time with stopwatch.
AI: Suggests PID adjustments if drone oscillates
Mathematics
Coordinates: fly to (3,2) (P5 Geo) Time: minutes & seconds (P4) Percentages: throttle 70% (P5) Estimation & rounding
Problem-Solving
Real-time spatial adjustment Trial & error with feedback loop
Creativity
Developing personal flight technique
Step 06
Battery Management & Flight Logging
Measure LiPo voltage before/after flights. Log flight times in a table. Calculate average flight time over 5 flights. Check battery temperature with IR thermometer.
AI: Creates flight log charts, predicts remaining battery life
Mathematics
Decimals: 11.4V vs 12.6V (P5) Averages: 5 flights (P5 Data) Bar charts (P4 Data) Temperature: max 45°C (P4) Fractions: 3/4 battery (P4)
Systems Thinking
Data-driven decision making Resource management
Phase 3 — Gripper Missions: "I'm grabbing things"
Mission 07–09
Step 07
3D-Print & Assemble the Gripper
Print 5 gripper parts (fingers, base, tendon guides, servo spools, landing extension). Assemble with ball bearings. Thread Dyneema tendon cables through guides.
AI: Calculates filament needed, generates assembly sequence
Mathematics
Volume & filament (P6 Geo) Tolerances: 0.2mm (P5 Decimals) Measurement: caliper (P4)
Creativity
Understanding mechanical design 3D spatial reasoning
Problem-Solving
Parts don't fit? Measure, identify, reprint
Step 08
Calibrate Servos & Grip Force
Set servo angles: 90° = closed, 45° = open. Test grip on different objects. Find the sweet spot. Update weight budget with gripper added (120g).
AI: Generates servo control code, suggests angle presets
Mathematics
Angles: 45°, 90°, 135° (P5 Geo) Variables: x = angle (P6 Algebra) Weight limit: 1130g − 750g = ? (P4)
Systems Thinking
Parameter tuning (angle → grip force) Feedback loops (grip too tight = crush)
Problem-Solving
Optimisation: best angle for each object
Step 09
Grab & Transport Missions
Mission sequence: ping pong ball (5g) → apple (150g) → water bottle (500g). Each object requires different servo settings and flight technique. Time limit: 1 battery.
AI: Tracks mission times, suggests strategy improvements
Mathematics
Division: 8 min ÷ 3 objects (P4) Probability: which ball? (P5 Data) Ordering: lightest → heaviest
Creativity
Strategy design under constraints Route planning & optimisation
Problem-Solving
Multi-variable optimisation Time & resource pressure
