For Parents & Schools

What your child actually learns while building a drone.

Your child sees missions, challenges, and a drone that grabs things. What they don't see: they're working through the Hong Kong Mathematics Curriculum (P4–P6) — without ever opening a textbook.

Every measurement, every calculation, every problem they solve has a real consequence: the drone flies, or it doesn't. The gripper grabs, or it drops. That's the feedback loop no worksheet can replicate.

Our approach: fun first, learning hidden.

They calculate because they must

Not because they should. "Will my drone take off?" requires addition and weight units. The child doesn't think "math" — they think "will it fly?"

Mistakes are flight data

A wrong calculation means the gripper misses. Not a red mark on paper — a real, visible result they want to fix themselves.

Hands before formulas

First they measure with a real ruler on a real part. Then — and only then — does the formula make sense.

No child ever sees the word "math"

Mission cards have a cool front (the challenge) and a hidden back (the curriculum reference — for you, not for them).

Mission progression

Difficulty and math concepts build gradually across 10 missions.

Mission 1–3 Beginner

Assembly — "I'm building a drone"

The child assembles the drone from parts. Every step involves measuring, counting, and weighing.

Addition Length (mm/cm) Weight (g/kg) Counting
Mission 4–6 Intermediate

First Flights — "I'm flying!"

Hovering, landing on targets, navigating a grid. Time pressure from battery life adds urgency.

Coordinates Time Percentages Division
Mission 7–9 Advanced

Gripper Missions — "I'm grabbing things"

Building the gripper, calibrating servo angles, calculating payload limits. Real mechanical engineering.

Angles Fractions Averages Weight limits
Mission 10+ Expert

Custom Design — "My own drone"

Designing custom shells, calculating filament, optimising thrust-to-weight ratios. Full creative freedom.
